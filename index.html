Aotearoa MediaProblem Statement

The Problem

  • NZ opinions are fragmented across Facebook groups, X threads, and private chats
  • No unified identity or home for Kiwi commentators
  • Newspaper opinion submissions are email-based, slow, and opaque
  • Toxic comments drive away quality contributors
  • Authors waste hours manually cross-posting to 4+ platforms
  • Great NZ voices never reach national audience
Viewpoint.nzVision

Vision

One place to write. Everywhere Kiwis read.

Canonical Home

viewpoint.nz becomes the permanent, searchable home for NZ opinions — owned by the author, not the algorithm.

Publish Once

Write in a clean composer. We format and publish natively to each social platform automatically.

Bridge to Press

Direct pipeline to NZ newspaper opinion desks, ending the email black hole.

Civility by Design

NZ-tuned profanity filter and moderation tools keep discussion constructive from day one.

Roadmap: MVP-First Approach

Phase 1 MVP (4–6 weeks)

Facebook + Instagram posting only, unified composer, basic reader page, comments with profanity filter ON. Purpose: live market validation in NZ before full build.

Parallel Track

User interviews (20+ NZ commentators), analytics instrumentation, newspaper editor outreach (Herald, ODT, Stuff).

Phase 2 (only if traction)

Add X, LinkedIn, Threads APIs. Cross-post reliability proven, then expand platforms.

Phase 3

NZ Newspaper syndication: RSS/email portal for editors, verification badges, republish workflow.

Phase 4

Full analytics dashboard, monetization (creator subscriptions), mobile apps.

Why MVP First

  • Validate demand: Do Kiwis actually want a single home for opinions?
  • Learn content patterns: What topics, lengths, and formats perform on FB vs IG?
  • Prove cross-post reliability: Meta APIs change often — nail one integration first.
  • Reduce API risk: Avoid building 5 integrations that may break or get rate-limited.
  • Gather real comment data: Tune NZ profanity filter with actual Kiwi conversations.
  • Avoid overbuilding: Ship in 6 weeks, not 6 months. Learn, then invest.

"We don't build the full publishing empire on day one. We prove one reliable pipe — Facebook + Instagram — then earn the right to expand."
